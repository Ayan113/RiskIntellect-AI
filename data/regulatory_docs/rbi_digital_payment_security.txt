Reserve Bank of India
Framework for Digital Payment Security Controls
Circular No. RBI/2024-25/42 — DPSS.CO.OD.No.629/06.08.005/2024-25
Dated: January 15, 2024

================================================================
SECTION 1 — SCOPE AND OBJECTIVES
================================================================

1.1 This framework applies to all authorized Payment System Operators (PSOs) and Payment System Participants (PSPs) engaged in digital payment services.

1.2 The objective is to ensure robust security controls in digital payment systems to protect consumers from fraud and unauthorized transactions.

================================================================
SECTION 2 — FRAUD RISK MANAGEMENT
================================================================

2.1 Fraud Monitoring Systems
a) PSOs/PSPs shall implement real-time fraud monitoring systems capable of detecting suspicious patterns.
b) Machine learning-based fraud detection models shall be deployed where transaction volumes exceed 1 million per day.
c) Fraud monitoring systems must achieve a minimum detection rate of 95% for known fraud patterns.

2.2 Transaction Velocity Checks
a) Systems must flag transactions exceeding velocity thresholds:
   - More than 5 transactions within 10 minutes from same account
   - Cumulative amount exceeding ₹2 lakhs within 24 hours
   - Cross-border transactions exceeding ₹5 lakhs in 7 days
b) Flagged transactions must be subjected to additional authentication or manual review.

2.3 Geolocation and Device Checks
a) Transactions from new devices must trigger additional verification.
b) Transactions from high-risk geographies must be subjected to enhanced monitoring.

================================================================
SECTION 3 — CUSTOMER PROTECTION
================================================================

3.1 Liability Framework
a) Zero liability for unauthorized electronic transactions reported within 3 working days.
b) Limited liability (max ₹10,000) for transactions reported between 4–7 working days.
c) Full customer liability if reported after 7 working days.

3.2 Cooling Period
a) A cooling period of at least 4 hours shall be provided for first-time beneficiary registration.
b) Enhanced cooling period for high-value transactions (above ₹2 lakhs).

3.3 Transaction Limits
a) Default per-transaction limit shall not exceed ₹1 lakh for new digital payment users.
b) Customers may request enhancement of limits after completing full KYC.

================================================================
SECTION 4 — REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================

4.1 Incident Reporting
a) All fraud incidents must be reported to RBI within 6 hours of detection.
b) Monthly fraud reports must be submitted in the prescribed format.
c) Cyber security incidents must be reported to CERT-In within 6 hours.

4.2 Customer Notification
a) Customers must be notified of all digital transactions via SMS and email in real-time.
b) Customers must be provided with a mechanism to report unauthorized transactions 24/7.
c) A dedicated helpline for fraud reporting must be available.

================================================================
SECTION 5 — TECHNOLOGY STANDARDS
================================================================

5.1 Authentication
a) Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all digital payment transactions.
b) Biometric authentication may be used as a factor.
c) OTP validity shall not exceed 10 minutes.

5.2 Encryption
a) All payment data in transit must be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
b) Payment data at rest must be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent.
c) Tokenization is recommended for card-on-file transactions.

5.3 API Security
a) Payment APIs must implement OAuth 2.0 or equivalent authentication.
b) Rate limiting must be applied to prevent abuse.
c) API calls must be logged and monitored.
