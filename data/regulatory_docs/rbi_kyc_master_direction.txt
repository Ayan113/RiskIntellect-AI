Reserve Bank of India
Master Direction on Know Your Customer (KYC) — DBR.AML.BC.No.81/14.01.001/2015-16
Dated: February 25, 2016 (Updated: May 2024)

================================================================
CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY
================================================================

1.1 Short Title and Commencement
These directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Know Your Customer) Directions, 2016.

1.2 Applicability
These directions shall apply to all Regulated Entities (REs) as defined under Section 2 of the Prevention of Money-laundering Act, 2002 (PMLA).

1.3 Purpose
The objective of KYC/AML/CFT guidelines is to prevent banks/financial institutions from being used, intentionally or unintentionally, by criminal elements for money laundering or terrorist financing activities.

================================================================
CHAPTER II — CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
================================================================

2.1 Customer Identification
a) No account shall be opened in anonymous or fictitious/benami name(s).
b) REs shall identify the customer whether regular or occasional, and verify their identity using reliable, independent source documents.
c) For individuals: PAN, Aadhaar, passport, voter ID, or driving license.

2.2 Risk Categorization
a) REs shall apply CDD measures based on risk assessment.
b) Customers shall be categorized into Low, Medium, and High risk.
c) Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) shall be applied for high-risk customers.

2.3 Ongoing Monitoring
a) REs shall monitor transactions to ensure consistency with customer profile.
b) Attention should be paid to complex, unusually large transactions.
c) Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) must be filed with FIU-India within 7 days.

================================================================
CHAPTER III — WIRE TRANSFER REGULATIONS
================================================================

3.1 Cross-Border Wire Transfers
a) All cross-border wire transfers of ₹50,000 and above must include complete originator information.
b) Beneficiary information must be maintained for at least 5 years.

3.2 Domestic Wire Transfers
a) For domestic transfers above ₹50,000, originator information must accompany the transfer.

================================================================
CHAPTER IV — SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING
================================================================

4.1 Definition
A suspicious transaction is one that gives rise to a reasonable ground of suspicion that it may involve proceeds of an offence, regardless of the amount.

4.2 Indicators of Suspicious Transactions
a) Transactions that do not appear to have economic rationale.
b) Transactions inconsistent with the customer's known sources of income.
c) Frequent cash deposits followed by immediate wire transfers.
d) Rapid movement of funds without clear business purpose.
e) Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions.
f) Structuring transactions to avoid reporting thresholds (smurfing).

4.3 Reporting Obligations
a) REs must file Cash Transaction Reports (CTRs) for transactions above ₹10 lakhs.
b) STRs must be filed within 7 days of identification.
c) Counterfeit Currency Reports (CCRs) must be filed for counterfeit notes detected.
d) Non-Profit Organisation Transaction Reports (NTRs) as applicable.

================================================================
CHAPTER V — RECORD KEEPING
================================================================

5.1 Retention Period
a) All customer identification records must be maintained for at least 5 years after business relationship ends.
b) Transaction records must be maintained for at least 5 years from the date of transaction.

5.2 Digital Records
a) Records may be maintained in electronic form subject to existing legal requirements.

================================================================
CHAPTER VI — PENALTIES
================================================================

6.1 Non-Compliance
a) Failure to observe these directions may attract penalties under the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 or Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007.
b) Officers found negligent may face disciplinary action.
c) Persistent non-compliance may result in license revocation.

================================================================
ANNEXURE — DIGITAL KYC
================================================================

A.1 Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP)
a) REs may perform V-CIP as an alternative to in-person verification.
b) The process must include live photograph and video with timestamp.
c) Aadhaar OTP-based e-KYC is permitted for accounts up to ₹1 lakh.

A.2 Central KYC Registry (CKYCR)
a) All REs must upload KYC data to CKYCR.
b) KYC Identifier (KIN) must be communicated to the customer.
